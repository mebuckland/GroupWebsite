Cardiopulmonary resuscitation
Learn the Skills

CALL 999 immediately

"If someone's had a cardiac arrest, they'll be unconscious, not breathing or not breathing normally. The person will die within minutes without immediate help. Act quickly and call 999 straight away."

How to identify cardiac arrest

PUSH hard and fast on the centre of the chest 30 times

"Place the heel of your hand on the centre of the person's chest, then place the other hand on top and press down by 5-6cm at a steady rate: approximately 100 compressions per minute. After every 30 chest compressions, give two breaths."

How-to guide to CPR

Give two RESCUE breaths

Blow into the mouth until the chest rises. Remove your mouth and allow the chest to fall. Carry on giving 30 chest compressions followed by two rescue breaths for as long as you can, or until help arrives. If you'd rather not give rescue breaths then call 999 and deliver Hands-only CPR
